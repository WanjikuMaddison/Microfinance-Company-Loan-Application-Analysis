# Microfinance-Company-Loan-Application-Analysis using Python
This analysis examines loan application data to identify customer patterns, loan preferences, and factors related to loan approval outcomes. Several useful insights emerged from the dataset.
## Key Findings
The first key finding is that loan amounts tend to be positively skewed, meaning most applicants request small to moderate loan amounts while only a few apply for very large loans. This suggests that the typical customer seeks manageable loan sizes rather than extremely high-value borrowing.

The second finding is related to loan repayment terms. The analysis of Loan_Amount_Term showed that longer repayment periods, particularly 360-month loans, appear to be the most common among applicants. This indicates that customers generally prefer longer repayment periods, likely because they reduce monthly payment pressure.

The third finding involves education and loan approval status. Graduates appeared to have slightly higher approval rates compared to non-graduates, although education alone may not fully determine approval outcomes. Other variables such as credit history and income likely play an important role.

## Recommendation
Based on these findings, two recommendations are proposed for the loan company. First, the company should continue offering and promoting long-term loan products because customer demand appears strongest for these repayment options. Second, the company should strengthen credit risk assessment processes and use multiple factors, including income and credit history, rather than relying heavily on a single customer characteristic.
